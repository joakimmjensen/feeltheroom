What I believe

The principles I've built everything on.

Not theories. Not things I read in a book. Things I've lived, tested, and come back to again and again.

"Energy is the true compass."
It's the most important ability I have — and the factor I trust most when making any decision. Before logic. Before data. Before strategy. I feel the energy of a room, a person, a conversation — and I let that guide me. It's not mystical. It's the accumulated signal of everything your subconscious has already processed. Learning to listen to it, trust it, and act on it is the single biggest edge I've developed in 13 years. And it's the foundation of everything F.E.E.L. is built on.
"Your daily battery."
Too many people show up to work running on 70–80% battery — and come home with 10–20% left. Work is not your whole life. Some of the most important things in life are waiting for you when you get home. Family. Friends. Yourself. They deserve more than the scraps left over at the end of the day. And so do you. Taking care of your foundation isn't selfish. It's the most responsible thing you can do for the people who matter most.
"Authenticity is the foundation of culture."
Don't put on a mask just because you walk through the office door. Be the same person at work as you are outside of it. When people show up as themselves — not a professional version of themselves — the culture becomes real. You can actually feel each other. Trust builds naturally. And that's when performance follows.
"The whole person — Sales & Leadership is a reflection of your own life."
How you show up at work is a direct reflection of how you live outside of it. You can't give energy you don't have. You can't lead with presence if you're running on empty. Sleep, nutrition, movement, hydration, emotional regulation — these aren't wellness topics. They're performance topics. The foundation of great leadership and great sales starts with how you take care of yourself.
"Performative balance."
You can't perform without having fun. And you can't have fun without performing. These aren't opposites — they're the same thing. A team that performs without joy burns out. A team that has fun without performing stalls. The goal is a culture where both are non-negotiable. Where the work is serious and the people aren't afraid to laugh.
Principles
"People buy from people — especially if I can make you laugh."
No matter the product, the price point, or the industry — the deciding factor is almost always the human connection. When people laugh, they relax. When they relax, they open up. Humour isn't a trick — it's a sign of genuine connection. And genuine connection is where sales actually happen.
"Sales is helping a person make the right decision for them."
Not for your quota. Not for your commission. For them. The moment you stop selling and start helping, everything changes — the conversation, the close rate, and how you feel at the end of the day.
"There is no work-life balance. There is just life."
If you need to draw a line between work and life, it means work is taking something from you rather than giving something to you. Everything you do should feel like a part of life — not an escape from it.
"Slow down to speed up."
Be aware of what you do and why you do it. We move too quickly and slowly lose the ability to pause and think. The ability to stop, reflect, and learn from the moments life gives you is one of the most important skills a leader can have.
"Great leadership is helping people lead themselves."
Leadership isn't about having the answers. It's about asking the right questions — the ones that help people find the answers themselves. Most people already know what to do. They just need someone who creates the space for them to hear themselves say it out loud.
"Hire very slow. Fire fast."
The best teams are built with intention. Take the time to truly understand who someone is before bringing them in — their values, their energy, their whole person. The right hire elevates everyone around them. They bring energy, raise the standard, and make the culture stronger just by being in the room. That's worth waiting for. But once you know it's not the right fit, don't wait. Waiting costs everyone.
