The 6 Social Needs

The Engine Behind All Behavior
1
Intelligence

The need to be seen as smart and capable. These people want to be the expert in the room.

→ Let them teach. Ask questions you know they can answer.

2
Acceptance

The need to belong and be included. Fear of rejection drives their decisions.

→ Create "we" language. Make them feel part of the group.

3
Approval

The need for external validation. They act to please and avoid disapproval.

→ Give genuine recognition. Their output follows your praise.

4
Power

The need to be in control and have influence over outcomes and people.

→ Give them choices, not commands. Autonomy is their currency.

5
Pity

The need to be seen as struggling. Victimhood creates connection for them.

→ Acknowledge struggle first. Don't solve before you empathize.

6
Significance

The need to matter and be seen as important. Status and recognition are everything.

→ Position them as VIP. Acknowledge their importance first.

F.E.E.L. for Sales

⏱️

Profile the prospect in 6 minutes

SMX: identify social need + decision-making style in the first few minutes of the call. Then build the entire pitch around what THEY actually care about.

→ Module 1: Feel — read the buyer before you pitch

Linguistic mirroring

Match the prospect's pronoun use and sensory language. If they say "I feel..." you say "I feel..." — not "I think..." Instant subconscious trust.

Information extraction that feels natural

The key is making people feel they offered information willingly. Information compounds: the more they share, the more connected they feel — and the more they keep sharing.

Reading buying signals in the body
