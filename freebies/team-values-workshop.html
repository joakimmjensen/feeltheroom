Team Values Workshop

A facilitation guide for building team values that actually drive behaviour — not just hang on the wall.
Most company values are written by management and handed down. Team values are built together. The difference: one creates compliance, the other creates ownership.
Values are identity, not rules

Rules tell people what to do. Identity tells people who they are. Peter Crone asks: "Who are you being — not what are you doing?" Team values work the same way. When a team internalises their values as identity — not compliance — they don't need to be reminded. They just act accordingly. Jim Rohn said you become the average of the five people you spend the most time with. A team with shared values raises that average for everyone in the room.

90–120 minutes
Whole team
Shared doc for collecting answers
Claude for synthesis
Before Before the Workshop
  • Block 90–120 minutes. No phones.
  • Prepare sticky notes or digital equivalent, a whiteboard or shared doc
  • Your role: facilitator, not decision-maker. Your opinion comes last.
  • Tell the team in advance: "We're building our values together. Come thinking about what matters to you."
Section 1 Opening 10 min
Say this (or something similar) "We're here to define how WE work. Not rules — but what we stand for when it gets hard. These values will shape how we hire, how we coach each other, and how we celebrate. Let's make them real."
"Think of the best team you've ever been part of — sports, work, anything. What made it great?"
Warm-up question. Everyone answers. No wrong answers. Don't rush.
Section 2 The Question Round 40–50 min

Go through each block together. Everyone answers out loud — or writes in a shared doc. Leader collects all answers. Don't edit or interpret yet. Just collect.

A — Identity Who are we?
  • 1Who are we as a team? Describe us in 3 words.
  • 2Who are we when things are going well?
  • 3Who are we when things are going badly — under pressure, when a deal falls through, when the week gets hard?
  • 4Who are we when no one is watching?↑
  • 5What would a new colleague notice about us in their first week?↑
B — Behaviour What do we do?
  • 6How do we help each other?
  • 7What do we never do to each other? What behaviour would be a betrayal of who we are?↑
  • 8When were you most proud of this team? What specifically happened?↑
  • 9How do we handle it when things go wrong — do we blame, or do we fix?
C — Energy What does it feel like?
  • 10What energy should there be in this team?
  • 11What should it feel like to come to work here?↑
  • 12What do we never want to become? What's our version of a bad team?↑
D — Together How do we spend time?
  • 13How often should we do something together as a team?
  • 14What kinds of activities should we do together?
E — The Individual Build the avatar
  • 15What are the most important qualities a person on this team has as an individual?
  • 16What is the one quality you personally would never compromise on?↑
F — Protection What do we guard?
  • 17What are the most important qualities to preserve as a team?
  • 18If you could only keep one thing about how we work together — what would it be?↑
Section 3 Synthesise with AI 10 min

Collect all the answers into a document. Then paste them into Claude with the prompt below. Claude will find the patterns, name the values, and describe them — in your team's own language. You then bring the output back to the room.

Claude prompt — copy and adapt
Here are the answers from our team to a set of questions about who we are, how we work, and what we value: [Paste all answers here] Based on these answers: Identify 3–5 core team values. For each value: — Give it a name (1–3 words) — Write one sentence that captures the core belief — Describe what it looks like in practice (bullet points) — Describe what violating it looks like — Write a short 2–3 sentence description in the team's own language Find the patterns. Use the team's own words where possible. Make it feel like them — not like a corporate handbook.

→ Bring the Claude output back to the room. Read it together. Does it feel true? Adjust the language until everyone nods.

Section 4 Adjust & Own It 20 min

For each value the AI proposed, ask the team:

"Does this feel true — or does it feel like someone else's words?"
Adjust the name or wording until it sounds like you. Aim for 3–5 values. More than 5 is hard to live by.
"What does this look like on a hard day — not just a good one?"
"What would we call out if someone violated this?"
Write both sides under each value. That's what makes them real.
Section 5 Test the Values 10 min

For each proposed value, ask the team:

"Would we hire someone who didn't have this?"
"Would we let someone go if they consistently violated this?"
If yes to both — it's a real value. If unsure — it might just be a preference. Keep exploring.
"If this value was part of our identity — not just a rule on the wall — what would we do differently tomorrow?"
If they can answer specifically — the value is real and understood. If they can't — it's still abstract. Keep defining what it looks like in practice.
Section 6 Lock It In 10 min
  • Final vote: everyone gives a thumbs up / thumbs down on the final list
  • If anyone is not fully behind a value — explore why. Don't override. Adjust.
  • Full commitment matters more than the perfect wording.
After Keep It Alive
In meetings Reference values in weekly team meetings. Name them when you see them.
In hiring Use values in interview questions. Would this person uphold them?
In celebration Celebrate specific examples of values in action — not just results.
In conflict When the team disagrees, go back to values: "Which of our values does each option honour?" It moves the conversation from opinions to principles.
In coaching Use values in 1:1s. When someone is struggling, explore which value is being tested — and who they want to be in that moment.
Revisit Every 6–12 months: are these still true? Do they still describe how we work?
Example Output Lunar Sales Team — Core Values

This is what the output looked like after a real workshop. The team answered the questions. Claude found the patterns. They adjusted the language until it felt true.

Authenticity
Core belief: We show up as we are. No masks. No pretending.
We don't want facades, stiffness, ego, or fake professionalism. We believe chemistry and realness create trust — and trust creates performance. Authenticity is the foundation of our energy, our humour, our collaboration, and our resilience.
Collective Ownership
Core belief: We win together. We take responsibility together.
We are at our best when we move toward a shared goal. This is not a blame culture — it's a "we handle it" culture.
Under pressure, we:
  • Focus on our circle of control
  • Take responsibility — individually and as a team
  • Handle things head-on
  • Are never afraid to ask for help
Energetic Positivity
Core belief: We protect the energy because it drives performance.
Energy in our team is intentional — not accidental. We understand that energy is contagious, and that negativity is costly.
What it looks like:
  • Banter and playfulness
  • Active breaks
  • Non-NPC behaviour — we bring ourselves, not a script
  • Celebrating small wins
  • Gamifying the simple stuff
Performative Balance
Core belief: We perform at a high level — without becoming rigid or ego-driven.
We don't choose between high performance and fun. We insist on both.
We work hard:
  • Strong work ethic
  • Ambition
  • Performance standards
But never without:
  • Humour
  • Genuineness
  • Relationships
  • Enjoyment
