The Pre-Meeting State Checklist

Prepare your state — not just your pitch. What you bring into the room matters more than what you've rehearsed.
Every salesperson prepares their pitch. Almost no one prepares their state. Your presence, your energy, your focus — these are what the customer actually experiences. The checklist below takes 5 minutes. It changes everything.
The story you bring in

The room you walk into is shaped by the story you're carrying before you enter it. If you're anxious, the customer feels anxiety. If you're desperate for the yes, they sense the pressure. If you're genuinely curious about them — that's what they experience too. Preparing your state is preparing your story.

Section 1 The Foundation Check — Physical
  • Sleep
    Did I get enough last night? If not — what do I need to do to compensate right now? (coffee, fresh air, movement)
  • Food
    Have I eaten in the last 3 hours? Low blood sugar = low presence.
  • Water
    Am I hydrated? Even mild dehydration affects focus and tone of voice.
  • Movement
    Have I moved my body today? Even 5 minutes changes your state.
  • Breath
    Take 3 slow, deep breaths right now. Feel your feet on the floor.
Section 2 The Mental Reset
  • Leave the last call behind
    Whatever happened in the previous conversation — it belongs there, not here.
  • This customer has never let you down
    Come in neutral. Come in curious.
  • Check your story about this customer
    "What am I telling myself about who they are, what they want, or how this will go — and is it actually true?" Set the story aside. Walk in blank.
Section 3 Intention Setting

Answer these before every meeting.

  • →"What do I want to understand about this customer today?"
  • →"What do I want them to feel during this conversation?"
  • →"What's the one thing I want to find out that I don't know yet?"
  • →"What story am I walking in with about this conversation — and is it serving me or limiting me?"
Not: "What am I going to pitch?" — that comes after.
Section 4 Customer Research 2–3 min
  • What do I know about their situation right now?
  • What's likely on their mind today — beyond our conversation?
  • Who are they as a person — not just as a buyer?
  • What would genuinely help them — even if it means not selling today?
Section 5 The Entry Moment
The first 30 seconds set the tone for the entire conversation.
  • Smile before you enter
    Physically or on a call — they hear it.
  • Open with genuine interest — not with your agenda
  • Your first question should be about them, not about what you're selling
Section 6 Post-Meeting Reset — Between Calls
  • Write down one thing that went well
  • Write down one thing to sharpen
  • Let it go
    The next conversation deserves your full presence.
  • If you got a no
    "What story am I telling myself about this no — and what would actually serve me more?" A no is information, not a verdict on your worth.
"You can't give full presence to the customer if your own foundation is shaky. Prepare your state. Everything else follows."
