How to Structure a Sales Call

The 7-step F.E.E.L. sales flow. What to do, what to say, and what to listen for — at every stage.
Most salespeople pitch too early, listen too little, and close with pressure. The F.E.E.L. flow is built the other way around: you earn the right to present by understanding deeper than the customer expects.

Step 1 Intro 2–3 min
Goal: Create safety and set direction
  • "Before I tell you anything about us — what made you say yes to this conversation?"
  • "What I'd like to do is understand your situation first, then we'll see if what we do is even relevant. Does that work?"
  • Your energy sets the tone. If you're nervous, they're nervous.
Key Q "What would make this conversation worth your time?"
Step 2 Dream 5–10 min
Goal: Understand where they want to go before you touch where they are
  • "If everything went the way you'd want in the next 12 months — what would that look like for you / your team?"
  • "What does success actually look like for you here?"
  • Don't skip this. The dream is the emotional foundation for everything that follows.
Listen The gap between where they are and where they want to be
Step 3 Current State 5–10 min
Goal: Map reality — what are they using, doing, experiencing today?
  • "Walk me through how you currently handle X."
  • "What's working well right now? What isn't?"
  • "How long has it been like this?"
  • Current State creates the gap. Without it, the problem has no contrast.
Listen Friction, workarounds, unspoken frustration
Step 4 Problem 5–10 min
Goal: Name the gap between the dream and the current state
  • "So it sounds like the main challenge is X — is that right?"
  • "Tell me a little more about that..." (not 'why' — 'tell me more')
  • Let them say it out loud. A problem they've named themselves is one they own.
Listen The real problem beneath the stated problem
Step 5 Probe / Elevate 5–10 min
Goal: Widen the gap — logical and emotional certainty
Logical
"What does it cost you if this stays the same? In time, money, team energy?"
Emotional
"How does that affect you personally?"
Future loss
"If nothing changes in 6 months — what does that look like?"
Identity
"Who do you become when this is solved?"
Key Don't rush to the pitch. They need to feel the problem fully before a solution lands.
Step 6 3-Pillar Pitch 3–5 min
Goal: Present your solution as a direct answer to their own words
  • "Based on what you've told me..."
  • Present exactly 3 pillars — each connected to something they said
  • Keep it to 3–5 minutes. The pitch is confirmation, not persuasion.
  • Don't add features they didn't ask about.
Key Every pillar should echo their words back to them
Step 7 Close — Pre-Close 2–3 min
Goal: Let them sell to themselves
"Do you feel I can solve your problem?"
→ If yes: "Why?" — let them articulate the value
→ If uncertain: go back to Probe
"What's the next step that makes sense from here?"
  • Avoid: "So, are you ready to move forward?" — too much pressure
Key You don't close, you confirm
Watch for Common Mistakes
  • ✕Pitching before understanding the dream
  • ✕Skipping Current State — no contrast means no gap
  • ✕Using 'why' instead of 'tell me more'
  • ✕Pitching more than 3 pillars
  • ✕Closing with pressure instead of a pre-close
  • ✕Talking more than the customer
