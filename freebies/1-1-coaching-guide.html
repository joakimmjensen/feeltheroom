1:1 Coaching Guide

A monthly deep-dive that goes beyond numbers — into what actually drives performance.
A 1:1 is not a status update. It's a coaching conversation. The difference: a status update asks "what happened?" A coaching conversation asks "what's going on with you — and where do we go from here?"
Before Leader Preparation
  • Review their numbers — but don't lead with them
  • Think of one thing you genuinely appreciate about this person this month
  • Prepare one coaching question based on what you've observed
  • Set your state: this is their hour, not yours
Section 1 Opening — The Whole Person 5–10 min

Pick 1–2 questions. Don't rush through all of them.

"How are you actually doing? Not the work — you."
"What's been the best part of this month for you?"
"Is there anything outside of work that's affecting your energy right now?"
"What have you been proud of this month — and what's felt hard?"
"What are you working on improving about yourself right now — not at work, as a person?"
The best performers are always working on something. If they don't have an answer, that's information too. (Jim Rohn: work harder on yourself than on your job.)
"If your colleagues described the energy you've brought to the team this month — what do you think they'd say?"
The Mirror question. Watch how they respond — with curiosity or defensiveness. The gap between how they see themselves and how they think others see them is often where the real coaching lives.
Section 2 Performance 10–15 min
"What are you most satisfied with this month?"
Start with what went well. Don't skip this.
"Where do you feel you left something on the table?"
"Your numbers show X. How do you read that?"
Numbers last. Let them analyze before you do. They usually already know.
Section 3 The Coaching Conversation 15–20 min

Dream → Problem → Solution

Dream
"If the next month went exactly as you'd want — what would that look like?"
Problem
"What's the biggest thing standing between where you are and that?"
"Tell me more about that..."
Not 'why' — 'tell me more'. It opens rather than interrogates.
"What's the story you've been telling yourself about this?"
This is the Peter Crone question. It goes beneath the surface — from the situation to the narrative they're running inside. The story is usually where the real block lives.
"Is there a conversation you've been avoiding — something you need to say or do that you've been putting off?"
Often the thing that's blocking performance is something unsaid. Give them space here. Don't fill the silence.
Solution
"What do you think would move the needle most?"
"What's one thing you could do differently?"
Only offer your own perspective AFTER they've answered. Ask first: "Can I share what I see?"
Section 4 Forward Planning 5 min
"What's your focus for next month?"
"What do you need from me to make that happen?"
"Is there anything you want me to hold you accountable to?"
Write down 1–3 specific commitments. Return to these next month.
Section 5 Closing
"What I want you to know is..."
Share your genuine appreciation. Make it specific to this person, this month.
"What are you most looking forward to this month?"
End on energy — not a to-do list.
After Leader Actions
  • 1 Write down their commitments and yours
  • 2 Note anything from "the whole person" section that you want to follow up on
  • ? Ask yourself: did I coach — or did I instruct?
