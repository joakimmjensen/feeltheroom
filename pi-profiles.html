Top 3 — B2B Salesperson

For Marcus & His Reps
1

Persuader

The quintessential B2B sales hunter. This is the archetype.
↑ Dominance ↑ Extraversion ↓ Patience ↓ Formality

Core Traits

  • Confident and competitive
  • Proactive — doesn't wait for leads
  • Connects instantly with new people
  • Communicates enthusiastically
  • Does not easily take "no" for an answer
  • Works best with commission structure

Why They Win in Sales

The Persuader is driven to win people over. They thrive in consultative, outside sales roles where relationships matter. Their urgency and initiative means they're always in motion — more conversations, more pipeline, more closes.
Watch out: Can over-promise. Dislikes admin, reporting, and process. Needs clear quota expectations or they'll pace themselves.

F.E.E.L. Angle

  • Natural rapport-builder — already uses Chase Hughes instinctively
  • F.E.E.L. teaches them to slow down and listen, not just talk
  • Biggest growth: Explore — asking vs. telling
  • Module 0 resonates hard: they know the pain of not being heard
2

Promoter

Persistent closer. Creative, people-first, wired to find a way.
↑ Extraversion ↓ Dominance ↓ Formality

Core Traits

  • Persuasive and persistent
  • Deeply people-focused and flexible
  • Connects easily to clients
  • Creative in finding paths to close
  • Adapts quickly to client needs
  • Motivated by recognition

Why They Win in Sales

The Promoter feels the room naturally. They are relationship-first — clients like them even when they say no. Their creativity means they find angles other reps miss. Less aggressive than a Persuader, but often closes bigger deals through trust.
Watch out: Can avoid hard conversations. May give too much ground on price to avoid tension. Needs coaching on standing firm.

F.E.E.L. Angle

  • Their biggest strength is already the F.E.E.L. framework
  • Needs training on Lead — when to take control
  • Chase Hughes' 6 Needs framework directly maps to their instinct
  • Elevate module: stop giving discounts, start elevating value
3

Maverick

Hunter mentality. High ambition, high risk, high reward.
↑ Dominance ↓ Patience ↓ Formality

Core Traits

  • High aspirations and risk tolerance
  • Visionary — sees the big picture
  • Aggressive new business hunter
  • Natural leadership instincts
  • Thrives in competitive environments
  • Often becomes an entrepreneur

Why They Win in Sales

Mavericks go after deals others won't touch. They see potential everywhere and aren't deterred by early rejection. They are the top performers on leaderboards — but only when they have enough autonomy. Micromanage a Maverick and they leave.
Watch out: Impatient with process and admin. May skip CRM. Will leave if they feel boxed in. Best managed with freedom + performance accountability.

F.E.E.L. Angle

  • Biggest gap: Feel — they move too fast to truly listen
  • Explore module is transformational for them
  • Chase Hughes: Maverick likely has Power as dominant social need
  • Frame F.E.E.L. as a competitive edge, not a soft skill

Top 3 — Sales Leader / Manager

For Marcus — The FTR ICP
1

Captain

The natural sales manager. Metric-driven, assertive, raises the bar daily.
↑ Dominance ↑ Extraversion ↓ Patience

Core Traits

  • Competitive and goal-obsessed
  • Charming but demanding
  • Understands process and structure
  • Strong communicator with the team
  • Constantly pushes reps to improve
  • Natural authority — graduates easily into leadership

Why They Lead Well

The Captain was often a great salesperson first. They understand the floor because they lived it. As a manager, they bring energy, urgency, and a results focus that keeps the team sharp. Numbers are their language — they turn KPIs into culture.
Watch out: Can be too results-focused and undervalue the human side. Reps may feel like a number. F.E.E.L. directly fills this gap: the Captain who learns to Feel becomes the best leader in the building.

F.E.E.L. Angle

  • This is Marcus — the FTR ICP
  • Module 0 Pain: brilliant at numbers, struggling to connect
  • F.E.E.L. is the missing half of the Captain's toolkit
  • Chase Hughes' 6 Needs: Captain's own need is often Power — use that self-awareness
2

Persuader

The pied piper of sales leadership. Reps follow them anywhere.
↑ Dominance ↑ Extraversion ↓ Patience ↓ Formality

Core Traits

  • Charismatic and inspiring
  • Genuine empathy for their team
  • Excellent coach and mentor
  • Creates loyalty and devotion
  • Teaches reps resilience
  • Always comes up swinging after a loss

Why They Lead Well

The Persuader-as-leader is loved. Their team would run through walls for them. They understand rejection because they've felt it, and they know how to pull reps up after a rough week. Their natural warmth creates a culture where people perform because they want to — not because they have to.
Watch out: May avoid difficult performance conversations. Can be too easy on underperformers. Needs frameworks for holding standards without losing connection.

F.E.E.L. Angle

  • Already strong in Feel and Explore
  • Biggest growth: Lead — holding standards with warmth
  • Elevate module: how to challenge without crushing
  • Chase Hughes: their own social need is often Approval — self-awareness is key
3

Strategist

The silent killer. Analytical, precise, and laser-focused on the long game.
↑ Dominance ↓ Extraversion ↓ Patience ↑ Formality

Core Traits

  • Results-oriented and analytical
  • Anticipates problems before they occur
  • Sees patterns others miss
  • Innovative under pressure
  • Individualistic in management style
  • Laser-focused on quota and pipeline

Why They Lead Well

The Strategist builds systems that make the team consistently good — not just occasionally great. They see 3 moves ahead. Their reps always know exactly where they stand and what needs to happen. Less emotionally available than a Captain or Persuader, but drives the highest long-term performance.
Watch out: Can feel cold or distant to the team. May underinvest in culture and 1-on-1 connection. F.E.E.L. is their biggest gap and their biggest leverage point.

F.E.E.L. Angle

  • F.E.E.L. is highly appealing to the Strategist's analytical mind
  • Frame it as: behavior data = competitive advantage
  • Chase Hughes' behavioral profiling speaks their language
  • Module 1: Feel — the most transformational for this profile
