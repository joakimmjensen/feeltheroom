00:00 — ~30 sekunder
Hook
Det første de ser. Afgørende. Giv det plads.

Most sales training treats the symptom.

pause

A new script. A new closing technique. A new framework for handling objections.

And you use it for a week. Maybe two. And then — slowly — it slides off.

Not because the technique was wrong. But because it was sitting on top of something that wasn't solid.

That's what I want to talk to you about.

Performance notes
  • "Most sales training treats the symptom." — Sig det. Kig direkte i linsen. Pause efter.
  • Lad "slides off" have luft. Det er det billede de skal sidde med.
  • Tempo: roligt og kontrolleret. Ikke sælger-energi. Tanker-energi.
2
00:30 — ~60 sekunder
Problemet med teknikker
Sæt scenen. Giv dem genkendelighed.

I've watched it happen. Good salespeople — smart, motivated people — who go to training, come back fired up, try the new thing... and six weeks later they're back to the old patterns.

It's not a discipline problem. It's not a motivation problem.

It's that the training never went deep enough.

pause

Because under every technique — under every script, every question, every close — there's a layer. A human layer. And if that layer isn't developed, the technique has nothing to attach to.

It's like building on sand. It holds for a while. Until it doesn't.

Performance notes
  • "It's not a discipline problem" — lad det lande inden du fortsætter.
  • "Building on sand" — du kan bruge dine hænder her. Fysisk gestus forstærker billedet.
  • Ikke anklageende. Empatisk. Du har set det. Du forstår det.
3
01:30 — ~90 sekunder
De tre fundamenter
Kerneindholdet. Tag dig tid til hvert punkt.

Over 13 years — across industries, across thousands of conversations — I've found that there are three things that have to be in place before any technique makes sense.

The first is energy. Being able to feel the room — actually feel it, before a word is said. Reading the person in front of you. Knowing when to ask and when to stay quiet. This isn't something you're taught in a sales course. It's something you develop.

The second is culture. What I call Performative Balance. You cannot perform without having fun. And you cannot have fun without performing. That sounds simple — but most teams are missing one side of it. And the leader is usually why.

The third is the whole person. Are you sleeping? Are you moving? Are you emotionally regulated when you walk into a room that matters? Because if you're not — no technique will take you where you want to go. You'll keep buying programs, keep learning new frameworks — and keep wondering why the needle isn't moving.

pause

These three things. That's the foundation. Everything else builds from here.

Performance notes
  • Brug numre fysisk — én finger, to fingre, tre. Giver seeren et anker.
  • "Performative Balance" — sig det langsomt og tydeligt. Det er dit koncept. Giv det plads.
  • "The whole person" — energien her skal være lidt mere personlig. Som om du taler direkte til én der genkender sig selv.
  • Den afsluttende pause + "Everything else builds from here" er et naturligt åndedræt-moment for seeren.

03:00 — ~90 sekunder
Adtool-historien
Moment of understanding. Konkret. Fortæl det som det skete.

Let me give you a specific example of what this looks like in practice.

I was working with a founder earlier this year. His company — Adtool — had gone from zero to 150,000 euros monthly recurring revenue in four months. They were preparing to expand internationally.

We sat down together. And within the first hour, we weren't talking about sales scripts or market entry strategies. We were talking about their core team values. About who they were hiring — not just their skills, but who they were as people. About what kind of energy they wanted in every room they built.

We talked about Performative Balance — that idea that performance and fun aren't opposites. That the best teams perform because they enjoy what they're doing, and they enjoy what they're doing because they're performing.

When the session ended, he stopped at the door. And he said to the next group coming in: "You're in for a treat. He's something else."

pause

That's the moment I knew what I was building was real. And why I had to share it.

Performance notes
  • Fortæl historien som en fortælling — ikke som et CV-punkt. Bliv i nuet.
  • "150,000 euros monthly recurring revenue in four months" — ro på. Det er imponerende. Lad det registrere.
  • Citatet — "You're in for a treat. He's something else." — sig det som du hørte det. Ikke med stolthed. Med stilhed.
  • Den efterfølgende pause er vigtig. Seeren skal mærke, at du mærker noget her.

04:30 — ~60 sekunder
Hvorfor mig — og hvorfor nu
Troværdighed. Direkte. Ikke hovmodigt — faktisk.

I'm doing this because I looked for this person for years. Someone who taught it this way — not just the technique, but the whole thing. The energy. The culture. What it actually takes to show up at your best, and stay human while doing it.

I couldn't find that person.

pause

So I'm becoming him.

pause

I've spent 13 years in high-performance sales and commercial leadership — across seven industries. I've closed million-dollar deals with some of the biggest companies in Europe. I've built and scaled five teams, across countries and functions.

And there aren't many people out there who combine the psychological side of sales, the leadership culture piece, and the whole-person foundation. I want to change that.

Performance notes
  • "I couldn't find that person. pause So I'm becoming him." — To korte sætninger med luft imellem. Ikke hurtigt. Lad dem lande.
  • Credentials-delen skal siges faktisk, ikke sælgende. Du fortæller ikke for at imponere — du fortæller for at de ved, hvem du er.
  • Energi: en lille stigning her. Du er ved at runde af mod CTA.

05:30 — ~60 sekunder
Hvem er det til
Specifik. Filtrerende. Giver de rette en følelse af at blive set.

This isn't for everyone. And I mean that.

This is for sales leaders who feel like they're constantly pushing uphill — and who know, somewhere, that there's another level available to them.

It's for founders who are building teams and want those teams to perform and actually enjoy doing it. Who believe that culture and results aren't opposites.

It's for commercial profiles who've sat in a meeting and felt — before anyone said a word — that something was off. Or on. People who feel the room. And want to learn to use it.

If you've bought courses before and something was still missing — this might be what was missing.

Performance notes
  • "This isn't for everyone. And I mean that." — kig direkte. Det skal føles som øjenkontakt.
  • Hvert segment (sales leaders / founders / commercial profiles) kan have en lille pause imellem. Lad dem tjekke om det er dem.
  • "If you've bought courses before and something was still missing" — blid tone. Ingen dom. Forståelse.

06:30 — ~45 sekunder
CTA — Request Access
Simpelt. Roligt. Ingen hype. Ingen pitch.

I'm building something. I know what it is. I know who it's for.

I'm not ready to talk about it yet — but I'm building it for the people who are already feeling what I've been describing.

Below this video, there's a form. It just asks for your email. No pitch. No spam.

If you sign up, you'll get something useful in the meantime — a tool, a framework, something you can use right now. And when the time is right, you'll hear from me.

pause

If this landed — you already know if you're the right person.

Request access. I'll see you on the other side.

Performance notes
  • "I'm building something." — simpelt. Roligt. Næsten whisper-energi.
  • "Below this video" — du kan lave en gestus nedad. Fysisk vejvisning hjælper.
  • "If this landed" — pause inden. Det er den afgørende sætning. Giv den plads.
  • Afslut med et naturligt smil — ikke forceret. Det du mener det.
