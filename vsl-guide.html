Setup
Before you hit record
30 minutes before recording
📷
Camera
Eye level. Horizontal. Close enough so your face fills ~60% of the frame. Tripod or stable surface.
💡
Light
Light from the front — never from behind. A window facing you is ideal. Avoid overhead light alone.
🎙️
Audio
Turn off ventilation. Close windows. External mic if possible. Test 10 seconds before you start.
🏠
Background
Clean and intentional. A room that feels like you — not sterile.
State note
  • You don't need to be perfect. You need to be present. There's a difference.
  • Talk to one person. A specific person you know — imagine them in front of you.
  • Record chapter by chapter. Not everything in one take.

00:00 — ~75 seconds
Start
Hook + the problem. Calm. Direct. No sales tone.
  • I have a hard time relating to other sales coaches or leadership programs
  • I've done SPIN Selling, Solution Selling, MEDDPICC, Situational Leadership, Authentic Leadership
  • They're fucking good at what they do
  • It just doesn't stick
  • The foundation is missing
Performance notes
  • "They're fucking good at what they do" — keep it neutral. Not an attack. An observation.
  • "It just doesn't stick" — pause after. Let them nod on the inside.
  • "The foundation is missing" — weight. Short sentence. Slow.
  • No salesperson energy here. Thinker energy. Like you're talking to yourself.
2
01:15 — ~120 seconds
Mid — the four foundations
The core. Take your time with each point. Use your hands.
  • That's why I've tried to conceptualise my 13 years of experience as a sales executive and leader
  • Energy as a Compass — Feel the Room
  • The Whole Human
  • Build Culture that Builds People — Performative Balance
  • How to lead with questions — whether it's on a sales call or in a 1:1 development setting
Performance notes
  • Use numbers physically — one finger, two fingers etc. Gives the viewer an anchor point.
  • "Performative Balance" — say it slowly and clearly. This is your concept. Give it extra space.
  • "The Whole Human" — slightly more personal energy here. Eye contact with the lens.
  • "Lead with questions — whether sales call or 1:1" — key point. It's what ties everything together.
  • You don't need to go deep on each point — tease it. They sign up to go deeper.
3
03:15 — ~75 seconds
Why me
Personal first — credentials last. Not the other way around.
  • First of all, because I really believe that this is essential in order to perform — as an individual or as a team
  • And because I hope that already now, you feel some kind of relation to me
  • I wasn't able to find this person back then — so I decided I'll become him
  • And then the numbers:
  • 13 years in high-performance sales and commercial leadership across seven industries
  • Closed million-dollar deals with the biggest companies in Europe
  • Built and scaled 5 different teams with +70 people
  • There aren't many people out there who've walked the talk and want to teach others how to
Performance notes
  • "I hope you already feel some kind of relation to me" — this is vulnerable and authentic. Stay there. Don't rush past it.
  • "I wasn't able to find this person — so I decided I'll become him" — pause before "become him". Two words. Let them land.
  • Credentials: say them factually, not as a sales pitch. You're telling — not selling.
  • "There aren't many people who've walked the talk" — your differentiator. Gentle but clear.
4
04:30 — ~60 seconds
Why you
Honest and filtering. Not for everyone — and that's a strength.
  • I'm not 100% sure who this will resonate with
  • I only want to work with people who this resonates with
  • I hope I can stand out as a little bit more different than the others
  • But I imagine it'll be either other sales leaders, startup founders or sales executives
Performance notes
  • "I'm not 100% sure who this will resonate with" — your strongest line in this whole section. Honesty beats polished pitch every time.
  • "I only want to work with people who this resonates with" — it filters the right people in and the wrong people out. That's exactly the point.
  • No hype. No "this is for YOU if..." list. Just an honest observation.
  • Energy: calm and slightly more personal than the rest of the video.
5
05:30 — ~60 seconds
CTA — Request Access
Simple. Concrete. End strong.
  • Below this video (point down)
  • If you decide to sign up you get: tools, frameworks, guides, workshops etc.
  • Dropping every 2nd week
  • Until you hear from me — when I'm ready to work
  • Once again — if any of this landed, you know you're the right person.
  • Request access. Drop your email. I'll see you on the other side.
Performance notes
  • "Below this video" — point down with your hand. Physical direction works.
  • "Until you hear from me — when I'm ready to work" — mystery. Nothing more than that.
  • "Once again — if any of this landed" — small pause before. Look directly into the lens. This is your close.
  • "I'll see you on the other side" — natural smile. Not forced. Mean it.
  • The last words are not a sale — they're an invitation. Hold that energy.
