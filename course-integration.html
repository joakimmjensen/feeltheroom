In Practice — Two Scenarios

What this looks like for Marcus

Scenario A: The underperforming Maverick

Numbers are down. The rep is disengaged. Marcus used to just talk KPIs.

1
PI: Maverick profile

High Dominance, Low Patience. Needs autonomy. Hates being micromanaged.

2
CH: Dominant need is Power

Don't tell him what to do. Give him the outcome and let him design the path.

3
SMX in the 1-on-1

Marcus reads closed posture + low eye contact. He doesn't ask "what's wrong" — he asks "what would need to be different for you to go all in this quarter?"

4
F.E.E.L. closes it

The Maverick describes his own solution. Marcus uses Ellipsis: "So what would you do?" The Maverick commits. Marcus never told him anything.

Scenario B: Hiring the next rep

Marcus has a gap on the team. He used to hire whoever felt right in the interview.

1
PI: What profile does the team need?

Team already has 2 Persuaders and 1 Maverick. The gap is a relationship manager — he needs a Promoter.

2
CH: Linguistic harvesting in the interview

The candidate uses "we" language and sensory words. Talks about client relationships before commission. Classic Promoter signals.

3
SMX: Reading their body

Relaxed, open posture. Lean-in when talking about customers. Nervous only when asked about individual targets. Confirms: team-motivated, not self-motivated.

4
F.E.E.L. closes the interview

Marcus uses Explore: "Tell me about a customer relationship you're proud of." The Promoter lights up. Marcus knows — this is the hire.

The Integration Rules

How to use this in the course

5 rules for keeping this clean

  • PI and Chase Hughes are tools, not modules. They are introduced inside F.E.E.L. lessons — not as standalone content. Marcus learns F.E.E.L. and picks up the tools along the way.
  • PI gets introduced in Module 1 only. After that it's assumed knowledge — referred to but not re-explained. "If your rep is a Maverick..." is enough.
  • Chase Hughes' 6 Social Needs is the most important concept to integrate. It runs through every module. Everything else from Hughes is supporting content.
  • Never cite Hughes or PI by name in the course videos unless it adds credibility. The concepts are what matter. Marcus doesn't need to know where they come from — just that they work.
  • The People Intelligence Stack is the internal framework name. Use it to build the content. Don't necessarily surface it as a term in the course — let it be invisible architecture.
