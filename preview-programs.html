Forslag 5

L-dualiteten — udnyt det dobbelte L

L = Lead the team (Leaders) og L = Lead the customer to a decision (Sales). Det er elegant — men det forklares ikke. En sektion der viser den dobbelte betydning ville gøre frameworket mere mindeværdigt og understrege at F.E.E.L. er én samlet tænkning, ikke to separate programmer.

Ny sektion i hero eller under comparison:

One letter. Two meanings.

The L in F.E.E.L. means something different
depending on who's in the room

L

F.E.E.L. for Leaders

Lead the team

Build the culture, the structure, and the people. Lead without control — create an environment where salespeople perform because they believe in it. Team values, cadence, onboarding, and shared goals.

L

F.E.E.L. for Sales

Lead the customer

Guide the customer from problem to decision — without pressure. Negative Reverse Selling, 3-Pillar Pitching, and the pre-close that lets the customer convince themselves. You don't close. You confirm.

Same framework. Same philosophy. The leader who learns to lead people — and the salesperson who learns to lead conversations — are both practising the same fundamental skill: creating forward movement without force.

The Ideal Sequence — sælg begge programmer til én kunde

Comparison-tabellen sammenligner programmerne, men ingen steder fortælles historien om at de hænger naturligt sammen: lederen lærer det først — og ruller det ud til sælgerne bagefter. Det er et stærkt argument for at købe begge. En simpel 3-trins sekvens viser det.

The leader already knows what "good" looks like in the customer conversation. That's the multiplier.
