01 — Energy
Feel the Room
Reading what's happening before a word is said — and using energy strategically
Frameworks
Polyvagal Theory
The nervous system scans constantly for safety vs. threat. A leader's regulated state IS the signal to the room. Before you read the room, the room is reading you.
Science
The Four Player Model
Every conversation has four roles: Mover, Follower, Opposer, Bystander. A trained room reader can diagnose which roles are missing — and why the room isn't moving.
Framework
Energy Leadership Index (ELI)
7 energy levels from catabolic (fear-based, reactive) to anabolic (intentional, creative). You can't use energy strategically if you don't know which level you're operating from.
Assessment
Thin Slicing
Humans make accurate judgments in 2-second "thin slices" of behavior. Reading the room in the first seconds of entering is a real, trainable skill — not intuition.
Science
Tools & Practices
Weekly Energy Audit
What gave energy this week? What took it? Over time, a personal energy compass emerges — a map of what drains vs. fuels you in real leadership situations.
Practice
State Shift Protocol
Intentional use of body, breath, and movement to shift energy state before entering a high-stakes room. Not a mindset trick — a physiological intervention.
Tool
Body Language Audit
Record a sales call or leadership meeting. Watch it on mute. Identify discomfort signals, energy shifts, and moments where the room changed — before anyone said a word.
Tool
Heart Coherence (HeartMath)
When heart rhythm is coherent, intuition and decision quality increase — and you can sense others more accurately. The physiology behind using energy as a compass.
Science + Tool
Key Experts to Reference
Joe Navarro — FBI
25 years reading people under pressure. Non-verbal intelligence as a professional discipline. "What Every BODY Is Saying."
Tony Schwartz — The Energy Project
"It's not time you manage — it's energy." 4 dimensions: physical, emotional, mental, purpose. Directly maps to The Whole Person.
Vanessa Van Edwards — Science of People
Most accessible bridge between non-verbal science and real-world performance. "Cues" — the science of charisma and first impressions.
02 — Culture
Performative Balance
You can't perform without having fun. You can't have fun without performing.
Frameworks
Learning Zone Matrix (Edmondson)
4 zones: Apathy (low/low), Comfort (safe/no standards), Anxiety (standards/no safety), Learning Zone (both high). Sustainable high performance only lives in the Learning Zone.
Framework
Radical Candor (Kim Scott)
Care Personally + Challenge Directly. Destroys the false choice between "nice leader" and "tough leader." The operational model for Performative Balance.
Framework
Five Dysfunctions of a Team (Lencioni)
Trust → Conflict → Commitment → Accountability → Results. The pyramid. Base: vulnerability-based trust. Fun cannot exist without it.
Framework
Belonging Cues (Daniel Coyle)
Laughter, eye contact, close physical proximity, energized short exchanges — these are measurable signals that a culture is alive. Fun is a belonging signal, and belonging drives performance.
Framework
Tools & Practices
"Hire for the Room You're Building" Framework
Define the culture you're building. Reverse-engineer behavioral interview questions that test for it — not just skills. Hire people who make the room better, not just fill a role.
Tool
Psychological Safety Survey (Edmondson)
Free, research-validated 7-item scale. Deploy before and after cohort to measure culture shift. Gives teams a shared language for what's missing.
Assessment
Radical Candor Feedback Workshop
Teams map their real feedback behaviors on the Care/Challenge matrix. Reveals the dominant failure mode — usually Ruinous Empathy in high-performing sales orgs.
Workshop
Key Experts to Reference
Amy Edmondson — Harvard
World's #1 researcher on psychological safety. Proves high standards + psychological safety are co-dependent, not a trade-off. "The Fearless Organization."
Daniel Coyle — The Culture Code
4 years embedded in Navy SEALs, Pixar, San Antonio Spurs. Three things all elite cultures share. The best book on what culture actually looks like in real time.
Kim Scott — Radical Candor
Former Apple and Google executive. The warmth/rigor model that makes Performative Balance operationally real.
03 — Human
The Whole Person
Sleep · Movement · Diet · Hydration · Emotional Regulation
Frameworks
The Five Fields of The Whole Person
Sleep · Movement · Diet · Hydration · Emotional Regulation — as a performance stack, not a wellness checklist. Each field has hard data connecting it to leadership output.
FTR Original
Allostatic Load
Accumulated wear from chronic stress and poor recovery. Leaders with high allostatic load make worse decisions and have lower empathy — measurably. The cost of ignoring the whole person.
Science
Emotional Contagion
Emotions spread from leader to team faster than peer to peer — amplified by hierarchy. A dysregulated leader destabilizes the whole team. Regulation is not personal wellness — it's performance.
Science
Energy Recovery Model (Schwartz)
Performance requires full engagement — which requires deliberate recovery. The best performers aren't those who work most, but those who oscillate most efficiently between exertion and recovery.
Framework
Tools & Practices
Pre-Meeting State Checklist
5 checks before any room that matters: Sleep ✓ Movement ✓ Hydration ✓ Blood sugar stable ✓ Emotional state ✓. Most leaders prep the agenda — not themselves.
FTR Tool
7-Day Sleep Audit
Track bedtime, wake time, energy rating, and decision quality rating for 7 days. Leaders are often shocked by the correlation. Based on Matthew Walker's research.
Tool
Somatic Check-In Wheel
Where are you in your nervous system right now? Ventral vagal (social, safe) · Sympathetic (fight/flight) · Dorsal (shutdown). Converts Polyvagal Theory into a daily leadership habit.
Tool
State Management Protocol
Physiological sigh (double inhale, extended exhale) + 60-second body scan + intention setting. Opens every cohort session. Models the habit while teaching the science.
Practice
Key Experts to Reference
Matthew Walker — UC Berkeley
Sleep deprivation costs $2,000/employee/year. REM sleep is the brain's emotional regulation mechanism. "Why We Sleep." Non-negotiable credibility on sleep as leadership tool.
Andrew Huberman — Stanford
Most listened-to science podcast in the world. Translates peer-reviewed neuroscience on sleep, stress, exercise, and breath into actionable protocols. Consulted with elite military.
Peter Attia — The Drive
Performance physician. Hard data on nutrition, Zone 2 training, sleep, and emotional health as interrelated performance drivers. For leaders who want the science without the fluff.
04 — Leadership
Lead with Curiosity
The leader who asks the right questions builds a team that can't stop thinking
Frameworks
Current → Dream → Problem → Probe → Solution
Your original sales and coaching framework. Dream comes before Problem — creating emotional investment before gap identification. Probe is the most skipped and most powerful step. Works in both sales rooms and leadership conversations.
FTR Original
System 1 + System 2 Decision-Making (Kahneman)
Decisions are made emotionally (System 1) and rationalized logically (System 2). Dream activates System 1. Solution speaks to System 2. Probe without addressing System 1 first is why most sales conversations fail.
Science
Multipliers vs. Diminishers (Wiseman)
Multiplier leaders (who ask questions) get 2x the output from the same people as Diminisher leaders (who give answers). Curiosity is not a personality trait — it's observable, teachable behavior.
Research
Humble Inquiry (Schein)
Asking from genuine curiosity — not diagnostic intent. Rebuilds trust and surfaces what's actually happening in the organization. The deepest level of reading the room is asking the question that makes the truth safe to speak.
Framework
Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan)
People are most intrinsically motivated when they experience autonomy, competence, and relatedness. A curiosity-based leader creates all three — simultaneously. Questions are an SDT delivery mechanism.
Science
Tools & Practices
The 7 Coaching Questions (MBS)
"What's on your mind?" · "And what else?" · "What's the real challenge here for you?" · "What do you want?" · "How can I help?" · "If you're saying yes to this, what are you saying no to?" · "What was most useful for you?"
Tool
Question Storming (Warren Berger)
Generate 50+ questions about a challenge before anyone is allowed to offer a single answer. Forces curiosity at a team level. Used at IDEO and Google. Direct cohort exercise.
Workshop
GROW Coaching Model
Goal · Reality · Options · Will. The foundational coaching framework — every question is open and curiosity-driven. Simple scaffolding for leaders who are learning to coach rather than direct.
Framework
The Advice Trap Workshop (MBS)
Identify your "Advice Monster" archetype: Tell-It, Save-It, or Control-It. Then practice replacing advice impulses with questions. 90-minute format. Immediately actionable for sales leaders.
Workshop
Current→Dream→Problem→Probe→Solution Triads
3-person role-play: leader-coach, coachee, observer. Practice the full sales/coaching formula with feedback loop. Both a selling AND leadership development tool in one exercise.
FTR Workshop
Key Experts to Reference
Michael Bungay Stanier (MBS)
Author of The Coaching Habit — world's bestselling coaching book (1M+ copies). "Stay curious a little longer, give advice a little more slowly." Curiosity as an operating system.
Liz Wiseman — Multipliers
Researched leaders at Oracle, Google, and others. Multipliers get 2x output. The research case for curiosity as a competitive advantage in leadership.
Chris Voss — Never Split the Difference
Former FBI hostage negotiator. Calibrated questions, tactical empathy, addressing emotional concerns first. Bridges Pillar 1 (Feel the Room) and Pillar 4 (Lead with Curiosity) directly.
